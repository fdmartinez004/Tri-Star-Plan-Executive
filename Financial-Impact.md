# 📊 Strategic Risk Governance & Financial Impact
> **Executive Summary:** Transitioning from "Security as a Cost" to "Security as an Asset" through the ROSI (Return on Security Investment) Framework.

---

## 📉 The Cost of Inaction (COI)
A single "Black Swan" event (Ransomware or Data Breach) carries costs that exponentially outweigh the Tri-Star Plan investment.

| Risk Category | Potential Fiscal Impact (Estimated) | Mitigation via Tri-Star Plan |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ransomware Payment** | $250k – $1M+ | **Prevention:** Identity Assurance (YubiKey) |
| **Legal & Forensics** | $100k – $300k | **Resilience:** Automated Response (CrowdStrike) |
| **Operational Downtime** | $50k/day (Classroom Disruption) | **Continuity:** Micro-segmentation (Aruba) |
| **State/Federal Fines** | Variable (FERPA/HIPAA Compliance) | **Governance:** NIST CSF 2.0 Mapping |

---

## 📈 Return on Security Investment (ROSI)
The Tri-Star Plan utilizes a mission-based ROSI calculation to ensure fiscal transparency for the Board of Trustees.

### **The Formula**
$$ROSI = \frac{(Risk Exposure \times Mitigation \%) - Cost}{Cost}$$

### **Strategic Objectives**
1. **Reduce Annual Loss Expectancy (ALE):** Minimizing the probability of successful phishing and lateral movement.
2. **Social Return on Investment (SROI):** Protecting the District’s public trust and ensuring student safety is never compromised by cyber-extortion.
3. **Insurance Premium Optimization:** Many cyber-insurance providers now require MFA (YubiKey) and EDR (CrowdStrike) for coverage eligibility.

---

## 🏛️ Board Governance Alignment
This framework aligns with **NIST CSF 2.0** and **COBIT 2019**, providing the Board with quantifiable metrics to measure District resilience.
